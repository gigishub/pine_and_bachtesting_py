1. The Search (Exploration)
What you do: Play with indicators, combinations, and filters on your In-Sample data.

The goal: Find a "Setup" that shows a statistically significant lift over the random baseline.

Your logic: "I see that when Relative Volume is high and we break a level, we win 5% more often."

2. The Hypothesis (The "Why")
What you do: Before moving to new data, you define the causal logic.

The goal: Ensure you aren't just trading a coincidence.

Example: "High volume on a breakdown represents institutional urgency; therefore, the move is less likely to be a 'fake-out' and more likely to reach the target."

3. The OOS Test (First Falsification)
What you do: Run that exact, frozen setup on the Out-of-Sample data.

The goal: Falsify the idea. If the edge disappears here, your "Reason Why" (Step 2) was wrong, and Step 1 was just noise.

The result: If it survives, you have a Validated Strategy.

4. Incoming Data (Live Falsification)
What you do: Trade the strategy (or forward-test it) on new, live data.

The goal: Monitor for "Model Drift." Markets change. A setup that worked in 2024 might stop working in 2026.

The "Parameter": You decide ahead of time what failure looks like.

Example: "If my Win Rate drops below 45% over the next 100 trades, my hypothesis is now falsified by the current market, and I must stop trading."
