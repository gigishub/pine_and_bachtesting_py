Step 1: The "Boolean + Ratio" Strategy
Define your entry filters as True/False toggles and set 4 distinct Risk:Reward ratios (e.g., 1:1.5, 1:2, 1:3, 1:5) as optimization parameters.

Step 2: The Multi-Asset/Timeframe Loop
Automate a loop that runs this specific optimization setup across a selection of at least 3 different pairs and 3 different timeframes.

Step 3: The Result Aggregation
Collect the top-performing parameter combinations (the "Winners") from every individual pair/timeframe run into a single master table.

Step 4: The Intersection Search
Identify which specific filter toggles and R:R ratios appear in the Top 10 results across the majority of your tested datasets.

Step 5: The Consistency Filter
Discard any "Winner" that only performed well on a single pair or timeframe, keeping only the setups that show positive expectancy across the entire matrix.

Step 6: The Out-of-Sample Finalist
Take the most consistent "Intersection Winner" and run it on a fresh dataset (data not used in the optimization) to verify it wasn't just lucky.
